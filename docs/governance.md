# PMO Governance Model

## 1) Control objective

Manage multi-subproject delivery through one control center:

- Unified target (schedule, budget, quality, collection, client)
- Unified rules (planning, cost, procurement, risk, reporting)
- Unified correction (detect, escalate, assign, close)

Baseline files:

- `master_objectives.csv` fixes total schedule/budget/quality/collection/client targets
- `escalation_matrix.csv` fixes decision boundaries and escalation SLA

## 2) Three-level structure

- Level 1: Master project level
  - overall objective, major risk, resource conflicts, key decisions
- Level 2: Subproject level
  - scope, schedule, cost, procurement, execution, acceptance, collection
- Level 3: Module level
  - contract, design, procurement, execution, quality/safety, handover, settlement

## 3) Core roles

- PMO Lead
  - owns total control rhythm and escalation
- Subproject Owner
  - owns site result and weekly submission
- Functional controllers
  - contract, procurement, cost, quality/safety, document control

## 4) Decision escalation chain

- Site-level can decide:
  - minor material substitution, same-day task resequence, small workface coordination
- Subproject owner can decide:
  - regular resource reallocation, short-term schedule arrangement, subcontractor site arrangement
- Must escalate to PMO:
  - total milestone impact, budget overrun risk, major design change, critical material delay,
    major client complaint, collection risk
